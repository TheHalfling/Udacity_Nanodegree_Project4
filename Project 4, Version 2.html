Reflection

Oregon has long been a Blue State when it comes to the Presidential elections, so finding that the Democratic Candidates had a greater support base was not unexpected. I was also unsurprised to see that the top counties and zip codes are those that make up and surround the Portland area as it is the most population dense. It was interesting to see that even in these urban areas there was still a strong showing for Republican candidates, which I found to be unexpected. Overall the information presented matched expected results and was an interesting peak into the financial side of the election.

One of the challenges with this data set was manipulating the date information to be able to group them and to show them in the plots in the correct order. This required learning some new techniques that were not covered within the online classes for the project.

I think an interesting further analysis would be to look within the top zip codes within the 4 digit extensions and determine if there are red or blue pockets that are clearly defined. You could carry that information over to all the zip codes and do a breakdown of the red vs blue contributions per zip code. One could use the ratio of democrat versus republican contributions to set different shades of color from blue through shades of purple to red.
